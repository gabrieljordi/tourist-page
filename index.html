Porto, Portugal

For those who like history:

Discover 3 unmissable destinations in Porto, Portugal.

With its unique charm and enchanting landscapes, the area is rich in culture, gastronomy, and colorful architecture, with houses built using the famous Portuguese tiles, in addition to the delicious Porto wine. There are many places in the city of Porto worth visiting, but we've selected three that are unmissable.

  1. Praça da Liberdade and Avenida dos Aliados

    Liberty Square and Aliados Avenue are two places you need to visit at least once while in Porto.

    Both are open-air and public, so there's no charge to visit them. On Aliados Avenue, you'll find an imposing statue of Dom Pedro IV in the center of Liberty Square. The architecture of the surrounding buildings is also noteworthy.

    The area houses several banks, as well as the Porto City Hall. As you stroll along the avenue, you'll also find several shops and restaurants.

  2. Miradouros do Porto

    Porto is a city with dozens of viewpoints that offer magnificent panoramic views of the streets, houses, and the Douro River.

    The most popular are:

    • Miradouro do Jardim do Morro
    • Catedral da Sé
    • Jardim do Passeio das Virtudes
    • Miradouro das Fontaínhas
    • Miradouro dos Guindais

    Depending on the number of days you stay in Porto, you can visit them all to get different views and perspectives of the city.

  3. Boat trip on the Douro River

    The Douro River is one of Porto's major tourist attractions, as it cuts through the city and is responsible for giving life to the place.

    If you want to experience something different, taking a boat trip along the river is an excellent option. Most trips last about an hour and cover various points in the city, offering panoramic and unique views.
